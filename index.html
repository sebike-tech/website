COMMUNITY HERBAL EDUCATION PROGRAM

Rationale

Based on the findings of the study, results revealed that Lumad adults in Glan,
Sarangani Province have high knowledge and frequent use of medicinal herbs, however,
moderate perceived usefulness and duration of use indicate a gap between knowledge and sustained,
confident application. These concerns affect the safe, confident, and effective use of medicinal herbs for health management.
To address this, a Community Herbal Education Program is proposed as an intervention designed
to strengthen proper herbal medication practices through culturally responsive education,
integration of indigenous and scientific knowledge, and community-based training.
This program aims to promote responsible use of medicinal herbs and improve health outcomes among Lumad adults.

General Objective

To enhance the safe, confident, and effective use of medicinal herbs among Lumad adults in Glan,
Sarangani Province through a structured community herbal education program.

Specific Objectives

⦁ To strengthen Lumad adults’ understanding of the proper uses,
benefits, and limitations of medicinal herbs.
⦁ To improve confidence in the safe and effective application
of medicinal herbs for common health conditions.
⦁ To promote correct preparation, dosage, and duration of medicinal herb use.
⦁ To integrate indigenous herbal knowledge with scientifically validated health information.
⦁ To monitor and evaluate changes in knowledge and medication
practices of Lumad adults after the implementation of the program.

Target Beneficiaries

Lumad adults in Glan, Sarangani Province who utilize medicinal herbs and would benefit from improved knowledge,
confidence, and safe medication practices.

Implementers

• Community Health Workers
• Barangay Health Personnel
• Researchers
• Local Government Unit (LGU) Representatives
• Partner Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs)

Duration of the Program

⦁ 6–8 weeks

Expected Outcomes

• Increased knowledge and understanding of medicinal herbs among Lumad adults
• Improved confidence in the safe and effective use of medicinal herbs
• Enhanced proper preparation, dosage, and application of medicinal herbs
• Promotion of responsible and culturally informed herbal medication
practices
• Strengthened community awareness and engagement in health
management using medicinal herbs

Monitoring and Evaluation

The effectiveness of the Community Herbal Education Program will be
measured through pre-assessment and post-assessment of participants’
knowledge and practices, observation checklists during workshops and
demonstrations, and feedback from participants, community health workers,
and local leaders. The data collected will be analyzed to determine the impact
of the program on participants’ knowledge, confidence, and proper use of
medicinal herbs.

Sustainability Plan

The Community Herbal Education Program may be sustained by
integrating herbal education activities into regular community health programs
and local wellness initiatives. Community health workers, local leaders, and
trained participants can continue conducting workshops, demonstrations, and
monitoring sessions. Partnerships with the LGU and NGOs can provide
ongoing support, resources, and updates to ensure the program’s long-term
impact and continuous promotion of safe and responsible medicinal herb
practices.
